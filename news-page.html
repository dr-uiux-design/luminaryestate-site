How to buy property in Bali

Bali has become a property hotspot in recent years, becoming popular with both property investors and buyers looking for a second home. Increasingly, investors are choosing to buy a house in bitcoins when choosing to invest in the real estate market in Bali.

It should be noted that foreign investors can purchase a house in bitcoins without any issue (indeed, Bali was one of the first countries where a real estate purchase was transacted in Bitcoin).

Learn more

However, buying property in Bali is not without its pitfalls. This article will highlight some of the issues buyers should be aware of.

Under laws in Indonesia which regulate property ownership, a foreigner does not have the right to own a property in his or her name (the right to own property is known as 'Hak Milik'). However, there are two ways a foreigner can purchase property in Bali.

The first is to contract an Indonesian citizen

  • The first is to contract an Indonesian citizen to act as a nominee to purchase the property on their behalf. After the resident receives the title deeds, the actual buyer enters into another contract with him, under the terms of which the Indonesian remains the owner only legally.

  • The second, and by the far the safest way to buy a house in bitcoins in Bali is by setting up a foreign owned company, through which the foreign investor can then legitimately acquire a Right to Build (known as ‘Hak Guna Bangunan’), and a Right to Use (known as ‘Hak Pakai’). A foreign investor can obtain a ‘Right to Build’ title for an initial 30 years, which then be extended for a further 20 years. Similarly, a Right to Use title is issued for initial 25 years, and can be extended until a maximum of 70 years.

With both these titles, they can be passed

With both these titles, they can be passed on to heirs under inheritance laws and transferred by sale to other foreign buyers or to local Indonesian buyers (in the latter case, they can then apply to transfer the title to a ‘Hak Milik’ title).

Having decided on the correct legal structure to make your purchase, the buyer then needs to conduct the appropriate due diligence with regards to the legal title to the property. As with all large financial transactions, irrespective of whether you choose to buy a house with bitcoins, or use a more traditional method of financing, it is highly advisable to use a lawyer to verify that all the documents are in correct order, and that full legal title will pass to the buyer.
